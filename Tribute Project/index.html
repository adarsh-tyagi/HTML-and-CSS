Dr. A.P.J Abdul Kalam

Missile man of India

Image of India's great scientist - Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam

Timeline of Dr. Kalam's life:

  • 1931 - He was born on 15 oct, 1931 in Tamil Nadu.

  • 1955 - He moved to Madras to study Aerospace engineering in Madras Institute of Technology.

  • 1960 - He joined the Aeronautical Development Establishment of the Defence Research and Development Organisation.

  • 1965 - He had first started work on an expandable rocket project independently at DRDO in 1965.

  • 1969 - He was transferred to the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO) where he was the project director of India's first Satellite Launch Vehicle (SLV-III).

  • 1980 - His research and educational leadership brought him great laurels and prestige in the 1980s, which prompted the government to initiate an advanced missile programme under his directorship.

  • 1981 - The Government of India honoured him with the Padma Bhushan in 1981 and the Padma Vibhushan in 1990 for his work with ISRO and DRDO and his role as a scientific advisor to the Government.

  • 1997 - He received India's highest civilian honour, the Bharat Ratna, for his contribution to the scientific research and modernisation of defence technology in India.

  • 2002 - He became the 11th President of India.

  • 2013 - He was the recipient of the Von Braun Award from the National Space Society "to recognize excellence in the management and leadership of a space-related project".

  • 2015 - On 27 July 2015, he died while giving lecture in Shillong.
